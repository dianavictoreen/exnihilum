Ex Nihilum means “from nothing”, an adaption of the philosphical argument by Parmenides, "Ex nihilo nihil fit," or "Nothing comes from nothing." Which is basically just to say that there is an origin, a story, a hand in every piece of matter that exists in our world. We hope to bring this awareness into our creative practices and those of our peers, so that we all might posses a sense of understanding, curiosity, and revere for the history behind our aesthetic desisions. In this way we hope to foster an increased level of intentionality in our creative efforts, especially in this world of mass production and consumption.
Ex Nihilum Collective was created by Victoria Luzuriaga and Diana Victoreen. Based in eclectic Brooklyn, the collective was born out of an urge to create a diy publication which merged a range of mediums within art, design, and literature, and to use our individual abilities and resources to provide an accessible platform for artists to exhibit their work, build connections within the comunity, and create/share work within a deinstitutionalized setting.
Our motives are simple: to build a community of connected individulas within the Brooklyn/ New York art scene. Our goal is to boost the work of our talented peers throughout the community, to make people stop for a second and appreciate something new and refreshing, and hopefully give them something to think about. We hope Ex Nihilum will be a bridging project between creatives, bringing people together, and spreading art-appreciation. We’ll be sprinkling the Ex Nihilum zine around New York City and beyond.
We extend our call for submissions far and wide, inviting creatives from all walks of life to contribute to our collection of art and to become a part of a group of creators seeking to connect with, support, and collaborate with one another.
Feel free to contact us with your art for submission, thoughts on our project, love advice, random facts, recipe suggestions, or just to say hello!
